About

Imagine Dragons is an American pop rock band formed in 2008, based in Las Vegas, Nevada. The band started out with Dan Reynolds and his group of friends in university with many people leaving the band and new people being recruited. The band had their first big break when they were called to fill in to perform in the Bite of Las Vegas Festival 200, performing to a crowd of 26,000 people. The band currently consists of lead singer Dan Reynolds, guitarist Wayne Sermon, and bassist Ben McKee. The band debuted in 2021 with the album Night Visions with songs in the chart-topping singles, holding the record for most weeks charted on the Billboard Hot 100.

The band finished recording their debut album Night Visions in the summer of 2012 at Studio X inside Palms Casino Resort and released the album in the United States on September 4, 2012. It peaked at No. 2 on the Billboard 200 chart with first week sales in excess of 83,000 copies, the highest charting for a debut rock album since 2006. The album also reached No. 1 on the Billboard Alternative and Rock Album charts as well as the top ten on the Albums charts of many countries. It won a Billboard Music Award for Top Rock Album and was nominated for the Juno Award for International Album of the Year. Night Visions is certified platinum in the US by the Recording Industry Association of America as well as many other countries. The album produced three tracks that reached the Billboard Top 40, four tracks in the ARIA Top 40, and five tracks charting in the UK Top 40.

Beliver by Imagine Dragons was released on February 1st 2017 and serves as the lead single from their third studio album, Evolve. It currently has about 2.9 billion views on Youtube and won Top Rock Song at the 2018 Billboard Music Awards.

Thunder by Imagine Dragons was released on the April 27th 2017 and is the second single of their third studio album, Evolve. It currently has about 2.3 billion view on Youtube and earned Imagine Dragons a Grammy nomination which contributed to them winning an award for Top Rock Artist 2018.
